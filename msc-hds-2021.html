Can Artificial Intelligence replace doctors?

What is “artificial intelligence”?
  • What is an “algorithm”?
  • What is “machine learning”?
  • What is AI a very broad area?
What can AI be used for in medicine?
  • Automate well-defined tasks (e.g. detecting lesions in a mammogram)
  • Supporting clinical decision making (e.g. risk prediction)
  • Providing treatment recommendation (e.g. recommender systems)
  • Direct human assistance (e.g. robotic surgery)
  • Direct human interaction (e.g. digital mental health, GP apps)
Can AI replace doctors? Issues to consider:
  • In what areas could AI achieve this? What tasks could it perform?
  • How would we implement this in clinical practice? i.e. integration in the care pathway.
  • How can we provide evidence that AI solutions are effective? e.g. do clinical trials work for AI?
Is it safe?
  • How do we make sure patient data is secured?
  • How do we ensure that decisions are sensible?
  • How do we monitor systems for performance?
Can AI systems make ethical decisions?
  • Will they make bias or exacerbate bias? e.g. socioeconomic, gender, racial.
  • How do we know if AI is biased?
  • How do we build “ethical algorithms”
